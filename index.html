I am Yiqi Liu (刘逸奇), and I usually go by Andrew. I am a physics Ph.D. student at Princeton University working with Jo Dunkley. My research sits at the intersection of cosmology, statistical inference, and large-scale scientific computing, with a focus on extracting weak signals from the cosmic microwave background.

Within the Simons Observatory, I work on foreground modeling and component separation for primordial B-mode searches. Recent projects include regression-based component separation, dust-complexity studies, and SO x Planck cross-correlation pipelines. My recent JCAP paper studies how dust complexity can bias recovery of the tensor-to-scalar ratio.

Before Princeton, I completed a B.S. in applied mathematics and statistics, physics, and mathematics at Johns Hopkins University, with a minor in computer science. There I worked with Tobias Marriage and Charles Bennett on the CLASS experiment, and with Nadia Zakamska on time-domain stellar astrophysics, leading to a first-author MNRAS paper on CSS1603+19.

I enjoy building tools that connect careful modeling with physically interpretable results. Outside research, I like rowing and alpine skiing.
